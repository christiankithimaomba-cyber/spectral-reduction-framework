\begin{abstract}
We present a complete synthesis of the spectral proof of Goldbach's conjecture, developed in Articles V.1–V.5. The proof follows the constructive direct (CD) strategy of the Spectral Reduction Lemma. Goldbach's conjecture is the fifth problem in the ordered list of **thirty‑three resolved** by the spectral method (entry \#5). We provide a correspondence dictionary linking the arithmetic concepts to spectral notions, and we integrate this result into the global corpus, which now includes BSD, Fuglede, Barnette and prime families. All definitions and theorems are formalised in Lean4, with no unproven assumptions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Goldbach's conjecture (1742) states that every even integer greater than 2 can be expressed as the sum of two primes. The spectral proof follows the constructive direct (CD) strategy: for each even \(n\), we construct a SAT instance whose satisfying assignments correspond to Goldbach pairs, build the spectral Hamiltonian \(H_n = \hat{V}_n + \Delta\) on the hypercube, verify the four pillars (P1–P4), and run the D‑RSP algorithm to extract a representation.

\section{Recap of the four pillars for Goldbach}

The spectral Hamiltonian \(H_n\) satisfies:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{P1}: Unique strictly positive ground state \(\psi_0\).
\item \textbf{P2}: Constant spectral gap \(\gamma(H_n) \ge 2\).
\item \textbf{P3}: Logarithmic area law, hence MPS representation with bond dimension polynomial in \(M\).
\item \textbf{P4}: D‑RSP algorithm extracts a satisfying assignment (a Goldbach pair) in polynomial time.
\end{enumerate}
