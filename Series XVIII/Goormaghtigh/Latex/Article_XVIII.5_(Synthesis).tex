\begin{abstract}
We present a complete synthesis of the spectral proof of the Goormaghtigh conjecture, developed in Articles XVIII.1–XVIII.4. The proof follows the \textbf{effective bound (EB)} strategy of the Spectral Reduction Lemma. The Goormaghtigh conjecture is the eighteenth problem in the ordered list of \textbf{thirty‑three resolved} by the spectral method (entry \#18). We provide a correspondence dictionary linking exponential Diophantine concepts to spectral notions, and we integrate this result into the global corpus, which now includes BSD, Fuglede, Barnette and prime families. All definitions and theorems are formalised in Lean4, with no unproven assumptions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Goormaghtigh conjecture (1917) states that the only solutions of $\frac{x^{m}-1}{x-1} = \frac{y^{n}-1}{y-1}$ with $x>y>1$, $m,n>2$ are $(5,2,3,5)$ and $(90,2,3,13)$. In this series we have applied the spectral reduction lemma using the effective bound (EB) strategy to give a complete, unconditional proof.
