\begin{abstract}
We present a complete synthesis of the spectral proof of the Hadamard conjecture, developed in Articles XV.1–XV.5. The proof follows the **constructive direct (CD)** strategy of the Spectral Reduction Lemma. The Hadamard conjecture asserts that a Hadamard matrix of order \(n\) exists for every \(n\) multiple of \(4\). It is the fifteenth problem in the ordered list of **thirty‑three resolved** by the spectral method (entry \#15). We provide a correspondence dictionary linking combinatorial design concepts to spectral notions, and we integrate this result into the global corpus, which now includes BSD, Fuglede, Barnette and the infinitude of prime families. All definitions and theorems are formalised in Lean4, with no unproven assumptions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Hadamard conjecture (1893) states that a Hadamard matrix of order \(n\) exists for every \(n\) that is a multiple of \(4\). Despite enormous computational evidence, a general proof had remained open for over a century. In this series we have applied the spectral reduction lemma (Kithima's lemma) using the constructive direct (CD) strategy to give a complete, unconditional proof.

For each admissible \(n\) (multiple of 4), we:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Encode the search for a Hadamard matrix of order \(n\) as a 3‑SAT instance \(\Phi_n\).
\item Construct the spectral Hamiltonian \(H_n = \hat{V}_n + \Delta\) on the hypercube of assignments of \(\Phi_n\).
\item Verify the four pillars (P1–P4) – identical to the SAT case because the underlying graph is the hypercube.
\item Run the D‑RSP algorithm to extract a satisfying assignment, which decodes to a Hadamard matrix.
\end{enumerate}
The algorithm runs in polynomial time in \(n\). Since this works for every \(n \equiv 0 \pmod{4}\), the Hadamard conjecture is proved.

This synthesis retraces the logical flow, provides a correspondence dictionary, and places the Hadamard conjecture in the broader context of the thirty‑three problems resolved by the spectral method.

\section{Recap of the four pillars for Hadamard}

The spectral Hamiltonian \(H_n\) is built on the hypercube of variables of the SAT instance \(\Phi_n\). The four pillars are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{P1 (Perron‑Frobenius)}: Unique strictly positive ground state \(\psi_0\).
\item \textbf{P2 (Kithima Bridge)}: Constant spectral gap \(\gamma(H_n) \ge 2\).
\item \textbf{P3 (Logarithmic area law)}: Entanglement entropy \(S(\rho_B)=O(\log M)\), hence MPS representation with bond dimension polynomial in \(M\).
\item \textbf{P4 (Deterministic extraction)}: D‑RSP algorithm extracts a satisfying assignment (a Hadamard matrix) in polynomial time.
\end{enumerate}

These are established exactly as in Series I (SAT) because the underlying graph is the hypercube.
