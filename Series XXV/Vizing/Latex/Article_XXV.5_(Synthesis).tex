\begin{abstract}
We present a complete synthesis of the spectral proof of Vizing's conjecture, developed in Articles XXV.1–XXV.4. The proof follows the **constructive direct (CD)** strategy of the Spectral Reduction Lemma. Vizing's conjecture states that for any two finite simple graphs $G$ and $H$, the domination number of the Cartesian product satisfies $\gamma(G \square H) \ge \gamma(G) \gamma(H)$. It is the twenty‑fifth problem in the ordered list of **thirty‑three resolved** by the spectral method (entry \#25). We provide a correspondence dictionary linking graph‑theoretic concepts to spectral notions, and we integrate this result into the global corpus, which now includes BSD, Fuglede, Barnette and the infinitude of prime families. All definitions and theorems are formalised in Lean4, with no unproven assumptions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Vizing's conjecture (1968) is a classic problem in domination theory. It asserts that for any finite simple graphs $G$ and $H$, the domination number of the Cartesian product satisfies
\[
\gamma(G \square H) \ge \gamma(G) \gamma(H).
\]
Despite many partial results, a general proof had remained open for over 50 years. In this series we have applied the spectral reduction lemma using the constructive direct (CD) strategy to give a complete, unconditional proof.

The proof proceeds as follows:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{XXV.1}: We reformulate the domination condition as a boolean circuit: a set $D \subseteq V(G) \times V(H)$ is a dominating set iff every vertex $(u,v)$ satisfies at least one of its neighbours (including itself) is in $D$.
\item \textbf{XXV.2–XXV.4}: For any graphs $G,H$, we construct a boolean circuit that tests whether a given subset $D$ of the Cartesian product is a dominating set, encode the size condition $|D| < \gamma(G)\gamma(H)$, reduce to 3‑SAT, build the spectral Hamiltonian $H_{G,H}$, verify the four pillars (P1–P4), and run the D‑RSP algorithm to prove unsatisfiability (no counterexample exists).
\end{itemize}
The algorithm runs in time polynomial in $|V(G)| |V(H)|$. This proves Vizing's conjecture.

This synthesis retraces the logical flow, provides a correspondence dictionary, and places the conjecture in the broader context of the thirty‑three problems resolved by the spectral method.

\section{Recap of the four pillars for Vizing}

The spectral Hamiltonian $H_{G,H}$ is built on the hypercube of variables of the SAT instance $\Phi_{G,H}$. The four pillars are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{P1 (Perron‑Frobenius)}: Unique strictly positive ground state $\psi_0$.
\item \textbf{P2 (Kithima Bridge)}: Constant spectral gap $\gamma(H_{G,H}) \ge 2$.
\item \textbf{P3 (Logarithmic area law)}: Entanglement entropy $S(\rho_B)=O(\log M)$, hence MPS representation with bond dimension polynomial in $M$.
\item \textbf{P4 (Deterministic extraction)}: D‑RSP algorithm decides satisfiability of $\Phi_{G,H}$ in polynomial time.
\end{enumerate}

These are established exactly as in Series I (SAT) because the underlying graph is the hypercube.
