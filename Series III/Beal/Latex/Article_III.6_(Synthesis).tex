\begin{abstract}
We present a complete synthesis of the spectral proof of Beal's conjecture, developed in Articles III.1–III.5. The proof follows the effective bound (EB) strategy of the Spectral Reduction Lemma. Beal's conjecture is the third problem in the ordered list of **thirty‑three resolved** by the spectral method (entry \#3). We provide a correspondence dictionary linking the arithmetic of Beal to spectral concepts, and we integrate this result into the global corpus, which now includes BSD, Fuglede, Barnette and prime families. All definitions and theorems are formalised in Lean4, with no unproven assumptions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Beal's conjecture, formulated by Andrew Beal in 1993, states that the equation \(A^x + B^y = C^z\) with \(x,y,z>2\) and \(\gcd(A,B,C)=1\) has no solutions. The spectral proof follows the effective bound (EB) pattern:
\begin{itemize}
\item Explicit effective bound \(N_0\) (linear forms in logarithms) such that any counterexample satisfies \(A,B,C \le N_0\).
\item Reduction to a finite SAT instance via a boolean circuit and the Tseitin transformation.
\item Construction of the spectral Hamiltonian and verification of the four pillars.
\item Application of the spectral SAT solver to prove unsatisfiability.
\end{itemize}

\section{Recap of the four pillars for Beal}

The spectral Hamiltonian \(H\) is built on the hypercube of assignments of the SAT instance \(\Phi_{\text{Beal}}(N_0)\). The four pillars are verified exactly as in Series I (SAT):
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{P1}: Unique strictly positive ground state \(\psi_0\).
\item \textbf{P2}: Constant spectral gap \(\gamma(H) \ge 2\).
\item \textbf{P3}: Logarithmic area law, hence MPS representation with bond dimension polynomial in \(M\).
\item \textbf{P4}: D‑RSP algorithm extracts a satisfying assignment (or proves unsatisfiability).
\end{enumerate}
